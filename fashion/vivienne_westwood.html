Vivienne Westwood Makes a Striking Debut in India: A Fusion of Punk Couture and Textile Heritage

In a landmark moment that bridges continents and cultural histories, Vivienne Westwood—the legendary British fashion house synonymous with punk rebellion and sartorial revolution—made its long-anticipated debut in India with a dazzling runway show at Mumbai’s iconic Gateway of India on April 1, 2025. Set against the architectural grandeur of the historic monument, the event wasn’t just a fashion show—it was a statement. A bold convergence of British avant-garde and Indian craft, the presentation paid homage to India’s rich textile traditions while retaining the rebellious DNA of the Westwood brand. The show unveiled the label’s Spring/Summer 2025 collection alongside select archival pieces, signaling both legacy and reinvention.

At the heart of the collection was a capsule line crafted from hand-woven Chanderi silks and Khadi cotton—fabrics deeply rooted in India’s artisanal heritage. The silhouettes—fluid sheer skirts, glimmering sequins, deconstructed tailoring—embodied Westwood’s iconic aesthetic while giving space to Indian craftsmanship to shine on an international stage. This wasn’t cultural appropriation—it was cultural collaboration.

The night also became a beacon for fashion diplomacy, attended by leading creatives, editors, and celebrities. Radhika Merchant turned heads in a breathtaking ensemble featuring a custom Chanderi sari paired with an archival 1990 Vivienne Westwood corset printed with François Boucher’s Daphnis and Chloe. Meanwhile, Kareena Kapoor Khan stunned in a crimson corseted gown with a thigh-high slit, seamlessly blending drama with elegance.

This show marks a pivotal moment in the growing conversation between Western fashion powerhouses and Asian creative legacies. In recent years, Asian textiles, craft techniques, and design languages have been increasingly celebrated—but rarely have we seen this level of intentional and respectful integration on a stage as global as Vivienne Westwood’s

More than just a fashion spectacle, this debut serves as a call to action for brands around the world to move beyond trend cycles and lean into meaningful cultural storytelling. With India’s fashion scene evolving rapidly—and its designers, artisans, and muses commanding international attention—this show felt less like a one-off event and more like the beginning of a deeper creative exchange. At Creatives Collective, we’re excited to continue spotlighting moments like this—where heritage, innovation, and artistry collide to challenge the norms and reshape the global fashion narrative.
